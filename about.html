Zhao Zhou is a Dutch-based installation artist whose practice critically examines the complex relationships between technology, standardized space, and human physicality. Working at the intersection of spatial design and immaterial media—primarily utilizing air as both medium and metaphor—from this emerge site-specific interventions that challenge conventional urban environments and embodied experience.

Zhou's background in interior architecture and furniture design informs his unique approach to spatial intervention and material consideration. His installations strategically transform underutilized standardized spaces characterized by industrial aesthetics that appear paradoxically static yet animate.

Through methodologies grounded in speculative design, established spatial flows are disrupted to reveal how different sensorial hierarchies govern movement and participation in contemporary urban settings. His work interrogates how technological standardization impacts bodily autonomy and collective engagement in increasingly automated environments.
