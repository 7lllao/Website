Although Zhao’s work does not provide solutions, he uses speculative design as a tool to investigate ideologies and ethics surrounding our urban environment, and where we are headed. With a background in Interior Architecture, he challenges traditional design norms by exploring the role of underutilized senses in shaping the identity of space. This perspective is informed by his astigmatism, which highlights the limitations of design approaches that prioritize sight and the able-body. Drawing a parallel between ableism and transitional spaces.

His astigmatism acts as a lens, bringing attention to infrastructures, affordances and sensory perception within transition spaces. These places, often devoid of identity due to their limited perception as solely functional and practical, are reimagined through immersive installations that offer new sensory hierarchies and grant the space an identity beyond pragmatism.

The work invites us to traverse and converse transitional spaces, challenging the status quo and prompting a more nuanced understanding of the role of sensory perception in shaping our relationship to the built environment. His practice is a bold and necessary contribution to the field of architecture and design, pushing boundaries and encouraging a more inclusive and holistic approach to the design of our urban environment.
